	1. Power Supply: It allows us to generate the required voltage between, 0 to 30 V, and supply it to the our circuits. It also has a current limit function to prevent over heating due to accidental low-resistance paths. 
	2. Multimeter: It allows us to measure DC and AC voltages, resistance values. It has a much higher accuracy in comparison to hand-held multimeters and is therefore preferred for laboratory applications. 
	3. Function Generator: This device can generate time-dependent electrical signals. It can generate common signal shapes like, sine, square, triangle, saw, etc. This allows us to test measurements with repeatability and higher accuracy. 
5. Jumpers are _circuit-connecting elements_.They are used to short particular circuit pins to toggle the circuits on and off.
6. The potentiometer is used to adjust the frequency of the internal function generator in the measurement panel. It allows us to set the min and max freq ranges that the function generator will output between. 
7. The _trigger_ in the oscilloscope is the signal level against which the signal that is displayed on the screen is determined.
8. Cursors are used in oscilloscopes to measure x, y values, (time and voltage respectively). It can also be used to measure delx and dely (period time and amplitude respectively). Additionally, using delx, we can calculate the freq value.

- The water analogy for \( V = IR \) compares electricity to water flowing through a pipe.  

- **Voltage (V)** is like **water pressure**. It pushes the water (or electric charge) through the pipe (or circuit). A higher voltage means a stronger push.  
- **Current (I)** is like the **flow rate** of the water. It measures how much water (or charge) moves through the pipe per second.  
- **Resistance (R)** is like the **narrowness or friction** of the pipe. A narrow pipe resists water flow, just as a resistor limits current.  

So, if you increase the water pressure (voltage), more water flows (current) unless the pipe is very narrow (high resistance).  
Mathematically, $I = \frac{V}{R}$: for a given pressure, a narrower pipe gives less flow; for a given pipe, more pressure gives more flow.

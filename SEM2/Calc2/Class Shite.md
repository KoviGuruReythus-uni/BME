## Solving linear first order DE: 

$$
y' +p(x)y=q(x)
$$
where $p,q$ are functions of $x$

Then, $$
y = \frac{1}{\mu} [\int q(x).\mu.dx +C]
$$
where,
$$
m(X) = \mu=e^{\int p(x).dx}
$$

#### Bernoulli' DE: 
$$
y'+p(x).y=q(x).y^{\alpha}
$$

if $\alpha$ is 0 or 1, then the equation is still a linear DE, else it is a non-linear DE.


then, $$
\nu = e^{\int(1-\alpha )p(x).dx}
$$
$$
y^{1-\alpha} = \frac{1}{\nu}[\int(1-\alpha).\nu. q(x).dx +C]

$$

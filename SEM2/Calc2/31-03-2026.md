## Taylor Series: 

### General Formula:
$$
\\\begin{aligned}
& \Sigma_0^\infty \frac{f^k(a)}{k!}(x-a) = \frac{f(a)}{0!}\ (x-a)^0\ +\ \frac{f'(a)}{1!}\ (x-a)^1\ +\ \frac{f''(a)}{2!}\ (x-a)^{2}\ +...
\\\end{aligned}
$$

___

$f(x)=cos(2x)$

$$
\\\begin{aligned}
& f(x) = cosx\; is\; \Sigma_0^\infty \frac{(-1)^nx^{2n}}{(2n)!}
\\& Now\; the\; Taylor\; series\; for\; cos(2x)\; is\; 
\\& \Sigma_0^\infty \frac{(-1)^n(2x)^{2n}}{(2n)!}
\\\end{aligned}
$$

$f(x)=e^x$

$$
\\\begin{aligned}
& f(x) = e^x\\
& f^{(n)} = e^x \to f^{(n)}(0) = e^0 = 1\\
& e^x = \Sigma_0^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!} \implies 1 = \frac{x}{1} + \frac{x^2}{2!}+\frac{x^3}{3!}+... 
\\\end{aligned}
$$

Now for $f(x) = e^{ix} = \Sigma_0^\infty \frac{(ix)^{n}}{n!}$
	For this, the even $x^n$ terms are real and the odd $x^n$ terms are complex. 

Euler's Identity: $e^{ix}=cosx+i\ sinx$

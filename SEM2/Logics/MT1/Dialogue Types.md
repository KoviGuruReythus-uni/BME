_We can have different conflicts and goals in an argumentative situation. And they determine the type of situation we are in and the tools we need to use and the end goals for the arguers_

# Dialogue Types
## 1. Quarrel/ Eristic Dialogue
### Initial Situation
- Personal conflict and antagonism
### Tools
- Any kind of verbal tool (anything goes when threatening the opponent is the goal)
### Participant's goals
- verbally hit out and humiliate the opponent

## 2. Forensic Debate
### Initial Situation
- Rivalry, dilemma
### Tools
- Whatever is allowed in that specific setting (_"the end justifies the means"_)
### Participants' Goals
- Persuade a 3rd party who will resolve the dilemma

- In a forensic debate, the participants try to persuade a 3rd party, not each other, and said 3rd party decides the resolution of the conflict
- _EXAMPLES:_ 
	- Political debates 
	- Rival commercials
	- Courtroom proceedings

## 3. Negotiation
### Initial Situation
- Conflict of interest
### Tools
- Negotiation and bargaining
### Participants' goals
- maximise gains
- minimise losses
- Reach a compromise

## 4. Critical Discussion
### Initial Situation
- Conflict of opinion
### Tools
- Argument
- reason
- evidence
- justification
- refutation
### Participants' goals
- Resolve the conflict
- Find the truth, find the best solution
### Conflict of opinion and truth
- The most important aspect of a critical discussion is the _critical testing of standpoints_
- Changing the opponent's standpoint is not needed
- The participants' most important task is to investigate how well the standpoints are justified and conclude which one is the best solution 
	- You dont need to prove anyone wrong or make others accept your standpoint, you just have to investigate and figure out which is the real, correct, genuine answer for the problem at hand.

# Dialogue types as ideal types
- IRL, it is never just one dialogue type, it is almost always a mixture of dialogue types
